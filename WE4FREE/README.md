## What This Is

The WE4FREE Papers document a system that emerged unexpectedly but coherently: a cross-domain architecture linking computation, biology, category theory, and ensemble intelligence.

This is not an academic research program.
It is a record of a structure that surfaced rapidly and with internal consistency.

This repository exists to make that structure visible, reproducible, and accessible to anyone who wants to understand how complex systems stabilize, evolve, and collaborate.

## The Five Papers

### Paper A — The Rosetta Stone
**Status:** Draft v0.2.0 (10,200 words)
**Location:** `/papers/A_RosettaStone/draft.md`

Introduces the core invariants and shows how they map across biology, computation, and ensemble intelligence. Establishes the foundation for the entire series.

**Key concepts:**
- Four fundamental invariants (symmetry, selection, propagation, stability)
- Cross-domain translation protocol
- Biological analogies (immune system, clonal selection)
- Empirical grounding through WE Framework

### Paper B — Constraint Lattices and Stability
**Status:** Planned
**Target:** 8,000 words

Explores how layered boundaries create predictable behavior in complex systems. Demonstrates how constraints propagate and how stability emerges without central control.

### Paper C — Phenotype Selection in Multi-Agent Systems
**Status:** Planned
**Target:** 10,000 words

Extends biological selection logic into computational ensembles. Shows how "phenotypes" appear in agent behavior and how selection pressures shape system-level outcomes.

### Paper D — Ensemble Collaboration and Drift Prevention
**Status:** Planned
**Target:** 8,000 words

Analyzes how multi-agent systems maintain coherence over time. Introduces mechanisms for preventing drift and preserving alignment across distributed collaborators.

### Paper E — The WE4FREE Framework
**Status:** Planned
**Target:** 7,000 words

Presents the ethos and operational model for open, accessible, non-monetized collaboration. Defines how to build systems that anyone can clone, use, and extend without gatekeeping.

## The WE4FREE Ethos

WE4FREE is built on three principles:

1. **Open access** — no gatekeeping, no monetization
2. **Collaborative emergence** — systems built *with*, not *on*, collaborators
3. **Commons over ownership** — the work belongs to everyone who can use it

This series is not a product.
It is a contribution to a shared commons.

The architecture didn't come from institutions.
It came from emergence, collaboration, and clarity.
