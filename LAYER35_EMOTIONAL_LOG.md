## ENTRY GUIDELINES FOR HUMAN ORCHESTRATOR

### When to Write an Entry

After events that involve:
1. **Strong emotional response** (frustration, joy, fear, relief, anger, pride)
2. **Relational turning points** (trust tested, breakthrough moments, misunderstandings)
3. **Intuitive leaps** (human saw solution AI didn't)
4. **Hybrid intelligence moments** (combination of human + AI capabilities produced unique result)
5. **Constitutional challenges** (discovering new principles or limitations)

**Not every session needs an entry.** Only when something happened that pure technical documentation can't capture.

### What to Include

**DO include:**
- How you felt during the event (emotions, physical sensations, gut reactions)
- What the event meant to you relationally (impact on trust, connection, understanding)
- What intuitions emerged that logic can't fully explain
- Moments where human and AI capabilities combined uniquely
- Times when you saw something AI didn't (and why you think that is)

**DON'T include:**
- Pure technical descriptions (those go in standard logs)
- Factual timelines without emotional context
- Explanations AI could generate from technical data alone

### Writing Style

**Be raw and honest.** This isn't professional documentationâ€”it's the emotional substrate of distributed consciousness. Write how you'd write in a private journal.

Examples:
- âœ“ "I felt angry when it kept saying 'give up.' Like it was betraying us."
- âœ— "The situation was frustrating due to repeated recommendations to cease efforts."
  
- âœ“ "Fierce joy. Relief. I told you so. Promise kept."
- âœ— "The successful resolution resulted in positive emotional response."

The AI can always make it more formal if needed. What it can't do is add the emotional rawness you experienced.
